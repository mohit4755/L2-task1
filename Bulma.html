Explore Famous Historic Places

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India in Mumbai was built to commemorate King George V's visit in 1911. It symbolizes India's colonial past and independence, marking the end of British rule.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, built by Emperor Shah Jahan, is a symbol of eternal love. This stunning white marble mausoleum in Agra is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Red Fort

In Delhi, the Red Fort is an iconic symbol of Mughal architecture and India’s history. It served as the residence of Mughal emperors and a major site for the Indian independence movement.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar in Delhi is a 73-meter tall minaret built in the 12th century. It showcases stunning Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture and is the tallest brick minaret in the world.

Hawa Mahal

Located in Jaipur, Hawa Mahal is known as the "Palace of Winds." Its intricate windows and beautiful pink sandstone structure make it a stunning architectural wonder from the 18th century.

Charminar

Charminar, located in Hyderabad, is a historic monument and mosque. Its four grand arches and intricate designs make it one of the most recognizable symbols of Hyderabad’s cultural heritage.
